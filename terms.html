Database Content Policies

1. Prohibited Information

Requests for database columns containing sensitive personal information are strictly prohibited to ensure full compliance with privacy regulations such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and other relevant legal frameworks. This includes, but is not limited to, the collection or inclusion of data such as emails, phone numbers, passport numbers, or other personal identification details. The only permissible personal information allowed in a database, in the absence of legal certification, is the client's name and surname. Any request for more extensive personal data must be accompanied by the appropriate legal documentation and certification that authorizes its use, ensuring that all data handling adheres to the highest standards of privacy and legal responsibility.

It is prohibited to request the following data:
Location
Email
Phone number
Social media handles
Postal code
Bank account numbers
Credit card digits
National ID numbers
Passport numbers
Medical records
Personal information of minors
Any other sensitive, traceable, or financial data.

Exceptions

The only exceptions to this policy arise when the client possesses valid legal certification, such as GDPR compliance or CCPA certification, that explicitly authorizes the use of sensitive personal data. In such instances, it is imperative that the client ensures their users are fully informed and have granted consent for the utilization of their sensitive information. This requirement is especially critical for industries such as private clinics, e-commerce, and any businesses that manage financial or identifiable data. To facilitate this process, the client must provide an official certificate or a photocopy of the relevant legal documentation in PDF format. This documentation must be submitted as either a photo or PDF document for verification purposes. It is important to note that we do not retain this documentation within our systems; it is utilized solely for the purpose of confirming its legitimacy. This requirement is integral to upholding our legal and ethical integrity while ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws.

Policies for Existing Database Modifications

1. Guarantee

Modifications to existing databases are covered by a 10-day guarantee, during which clients can request additional changes free of charge. It is crucial to understand that this guarantee may be voided if the client independently alters the database once it has been delivered. Clients must agree to our terms and conditions before initiating any modifications, as this ensures mutual understanding and accountability throughout the process.

2. Recommendation

We strongly recommend that clients maintain a backup of their database prior to requesting any changes. Modifying a previously used database without careful consideration and a well-planned approach may lead to unforeseen issues. While we will not add anything that the client has not specifically requested, it is essential to acknowledge that unauthorized modifications may impact the database's functionality and optimal usage. Our team is available to provide guidance and suggest potential adjustments that align with the client's needs while safeguarding the original data structure and design. However, the client assumes full responsibility for any complications that may arise from changes made to the database. We are committed to delivering professional work and will address any inquiries, suggestions, and requests. We will also provide recommendations to ensure that the changes do not disrupt the hierarchical schema or hinder data manipulation and analysis.

3. Accepted Formats

We only modify MySQL and SQLite databases. All files must be submitted in their original export format, compressed into a ZIP file. We do not require direct access to the client's database server for modifications, streamlining the process for both parties. It is advisable to send the database in an unencrypted ZIP format to facilitate modifications according to the client's specifications.

4. Client Responsibility

It is the client’s responsibility to ensure compliance with legal requirements when it comes to sensitive data. Adding sensitive information to the database is strictly prohibited unless the client possesses valid certification or permission verifying their legal right to use such data. This certification must demonstrate that the client has obtained the necessary consent from users regarding the use of their sensitive information.

5. Liability Disclaimer

Neither our company nor Aaron Obando, the founder, will be held liable for any oversight or data loss resulting from modifications, the failure to conduct backups, security vulnerabilities leading to data breaches, or any cyberattacks that may occur during or after the modification process. Clients must prioritize data protection and implement adequate security measures to safeguard their information.

6. Modification Fees

The cost for modifications is structured based on the complexity and size of the database. For databases with 5 to 8 tables or approximately 5,000 records, the fee is $149. For those with 8 to 12 tables or around 25,000 records, the charge increases to $849. For databases exceeding 12 tables and classified as intensive or enterprise use (with more than 60,000 records), the fee is set at $1,999. These prices reflect the intricacies of the work and the risks involved. Clients acknowledge and accept these terms upon proceeding with modifications.

7. Cancellation and Refunds

This service incurs a 15% penalty for refunds, applicable both for cancellations prior to the initiation of work and for completed work. This measure is instituted to ensure the integrity of our business. 8. Commitment to Client Satisfaction

Our dedication is to maintain client satisfaction and the optimal functioning of their databases. Clients need not worry about losing their work, as we will perform regular backups, including any changes made that the client approves. The special guarantee for this service allows for up to 10 changes within the 10-day window. However, this guarantee will be voided if the client alters the database after delivery. Therefore, we encourage clients to thoroughly read and understand these policies before proceeding with any modifications.

9. Modification of New or Unused Databases from Other Providers

If the database being modified is an existing one that was acquired from another provider or developer and has not been used prior or contains between 0 to 2000 records, the modification fee will be set at $119. This applies only to databases from other providers or developers. In the case of our databases, the extension fee must be paid separately, as previously mentioned. This modification will still be subject to the guarantee provided under this policy, allowing for a maximum of 5 changes within the specified timeframe. As previously indicated, the guarantee may be voided if the client modifies the database independently during the guarantee period. Additionally, a refund penalty of 10% will apply in this case. Clients are encouraged to review the terms and conditions applicable to these modifications to ensure compliance and understanding of the process.
