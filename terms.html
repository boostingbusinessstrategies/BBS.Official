Database Content Policies

1. Prohibited Information

Requests for database columns containing sensitive personal information are strictly prohibited to ensure full compliance with privacy regulations such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), and other relevant legal frameworks. This includes, but is not limited to, the collection or inclusion of data such as emails, phone numbers, passport numbers, or other personal identification details. For healthcare-related industries, particularly private clinics, the HIPAA regulations apply to any request involving health-related data, ensuring that sensitive health information is managed in accordance with HIPAA's strict privacy and security requirements.

The only permissible personal information allowed in a database, in the absence of legal certification, is the client's name and surname. Any request for more extensive personal data, including health information governed by HIPAA, must be accompanied by the appropriate legal documentation and certification that authorizes its use, ensuring that all data handling adheres to the highest standards of privacy and legal responsibility.

It is prohibited to request the following data:
1. Location
2. Email
3. Phone number
4. Social media handles
5. Postal code
6. Bank account numbers
7. Credit card digits
8. National ID numbers
9. Passport numbers
10. Medical records
11. Personal information of minors
Any other sensitive, traceable, or financial data.

Exceptions

The only exceptions to this policy arise when the client possesses valid legal certification, such as GDPR compliance, CCPA certification, or HIPAA compliance for healthcare-related entities. The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) establishes stringent requirements for the protection of sensitive health information, particularly for industries such as private clinics and any business handling electronic health records (EHR). In such cases, it is imperative that the client ensures their users are fully informed and have granted explicit consent for the utilization of their sensitive health data.

This requirement is especially critical for sectors such as private medical clinics, e-commerce, and businesses managing financial or identifiable personal data. To facilitate this process, the client must provide an official certificate or a photocopy of the relevant legal documentation—whether GDPR, CCPA, or HIPAA—in PDF format. This documentation must be submitted as either a photo or PDF document for verification purposes. It is important to note that we do not retain this documentation within our systems; it is solely used for the purpose of confirming its legitimacy. This policy ensures that we maintain compliance with applicable data protection laws and uphold our legal and ethical integrity, particularly when managing health information under HIPAA’s strict privacy and security standards.
